Introduction

Even with Hitler defeated and the war in the European Theatre over, President Truman and his administration saw no end in the war in the Pacific. As a measure to quickly end the war, Truman made the contriversial decision of dropping the first two atomic bombs ever on the cities of Hiroshima and Nagasaki, marking the end of the official end of the Second World War.

Model United Nations (MUN) is a program that allows students to take part in UN simulations. Participants, or delegates, represent countries in various international/regional bodies (UN Security Council, NATO, etc.) and discuss a specific issue relevant to that body. Students learn about international relations, current events, and the UN while developing valuable leadership and communication skills. At a conference, delegates apply their problem solving, negotiation, and debate skills to solve the most important issues facing the global community.
